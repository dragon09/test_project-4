4.0 out of 5 starsPros and Cons
By Ace of Knaves on December 9, 2014
I bought this from the Lego store in Florida, and really enjoyed putting it together. Looks great with my Ultimate Lego Batmobile.

The Cons:

It's kind of flimsy. If you have the Ultimate Lego Batmobile, you know how that thing is *solid*. With this, a lot of the armor/flap pieces are just itching to fall off. Not that I'd drive it down the stairs or anything, but this one really is a shelf piece.

With the way some of the pieces are angled, they don't really "snap" into place. They secure in one spot, then you just kind of eyeball them to best fit. The large side panels behind the cockpit are the primary example of this.

It doesn't "do anything". With the Ultimate Batmobile, turning the steering wheel opens a "shield' on the front, revealing the engine. Spinning the tires engages the "drivetrain", and spins all sorts of engine parts, gears, and the flames from the back. The Tumbler doesn't have anything like that.

The Pros:

Lots of pieces. This one is going to keep you busy for a while. The cockpit is very detailed, especially considering that you can't really see it once you put the roof panels on.

It's huge. While it is about the same length as the Ultimate Batmobile, it's much bulkier and impressive looking. The tires alone (which are rubber) scream "All your other Lego sets? I'm going to drive over them".

It's awesome. Can't really qualify this one. Speaks for itself. None of the cons matter - once you put it together, it's just awesome.

5.0 out of 5 starsAbsolutely Incredible - Well Worth the Price
By barrosd12 on October 26, 2015
Verified Purchase
So this was my first real "big boy" LEGO set. I plunked down $200 on LEGO. I didn't even tell my wife how much this thing cost because I knew she'd look at me like I was crazy (but also love the set once built). I had no idea what I was getting myself into.

I had an entire evening and morning to myself and decided I would watch all three of the Nolan Batman movies while building this colossal set. I thought I'd maybe make it through the first one and get halfway through Dark Knight. I actually watched all 3 movies to completion and still needed a short TV show to finish it out. It's easily an 8-10 hour build depending on your level of expertise and your current abilities in spotting tiny pieces.

The set is incredibly intricate. There are 5 books to work your way through and each section is incredibly well thought out in terms of build quality and creativity of each individual section. You'll rarely repeat the same thing twice.

Overall, I highly recommend this set, especially if you love LEGOs as much as I do.
